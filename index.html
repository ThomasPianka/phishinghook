Introduction

In today's digital landscape, the uptake in phishing scams has increased the risk for data theft, particularly for those not primed to recognize deceptive emails and advertisements. With vulnerable populationsâ€™ risk growing due to new and subtle phishing techniques, effective tools that detect danger before it is too late are required to maintain online safety. While existing phishing detectors offer valuable protection, there is a growing need to include a confidence metric for each suspicious link analyzed. Existing detectors may identify false trends such as flagging redirect service links like bit.ly or incorrectly advise users to avoid websites that are not necessarily unsafe, such as personal or dated websites. The need for an accurate and holistic approach is required to browse the Internet without restrictions and concern, while also maintaining the integrity of personal data.
